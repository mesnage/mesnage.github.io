Toxicology Advice & Consulting

I use my experience as a toxicologist to provide expert consulting on the risk assessment of pesticides. In 2018, I served as a consultant on glyphosate risk assessment issues as part of litigation in the U.S. over glyphosate health effects. I also work as a scientific consultant for the Heartland study to help understand if herbicide exposure may trigger heritable epigenetic changes that influence development
I am also a council member at Gerson Lehrman Group (GLG).

Genomics & Bioinformatics

When I am not working on research projects at King’s College London, I am also providing expert consulting services in toxicology and bioinformatics.
To complement my PhD in molecular biology, I recently undertook a MSc in Bioinformatics at Birkbeck University of London which was awarded with the highest distinction. Undertaking a Masters course on bioinformatics allowed me to familiarise myself with the use of modern technologies in the medical sector. This includes core concepts and statistical fundamentals underpinning bioinformatics. I learned how to program in Python and design and query databases using SQL, and gain experience in more advanced programming practices such as software testing and application development.
I am using this combination of skills to help clinicians analyse complex biological data from molecular profiling “omics” technologies, such as high-throughput DNA sequencing analysis of the gut microbiome. My inspiration is to develop and promote the use of deep molecular phenotyping approaches in day-to-day clinical practices.
I use the most recent bioinformatics pipelines in conjunction with advanced statistical programming methods to convert these into clinically-useful knowledge including:

- Analysis of microbiome composition and function (both amplicon-based and shotgun metagenomics)
- Processing of RNA-sequencing data (based on the Tuxedo protocol)
- Use of machine learning tools (e.g. regression methods, random forests)
I can also create 'shiny' applications to provide user friendly versions of complex statistical analysis. In an application deployed on R shiny servers, I use a publicly available dataset from a study in which a range of demographic and clinical parameters are used to predict if a patient has diabetes. I build a simple model to demonstrate how it is possible to create an application which can be used to predict a clinical outcome based on baseline parameters entered by a user, with a machine learning algorithm running in the background to automatically update the predictions based on the user's input. Feel free to contact me if you need any further information.
