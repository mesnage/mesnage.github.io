Research

Researcher at King’s College London in the Department of Medical & Molecular Genetics, I analyse data produced with high throughput biology techniques to understand the effects of pesticides on the gut microbiome.

My research focus over the last 10 years has been on the safety evaluation of environmental contaminants, including pesticides, publishing around 50 scientific articles on this subject.

Scientific consulting

When I am not working on research projects at King’s College London, I am also providing expert consulting services in toxicology and bioinformatics.

I am scientific consultant for Buchinger Wilhelmi clinic in Germany, which specialises in therapeutic fasting, where I analyse clinical data in order to predict response to fasting based on individual characteristics. I also work as a scientific consultant for the Heartland study to help understand if herbicide exposure may trigger heritable epigenetic changes that influence child development.
